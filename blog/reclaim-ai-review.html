Reclaim.ai Review: The Calendar That Says "No" For You

Stop playing Calendar Tetris. Reclaim.ai is the smart calendar that automatically blocks time for your tasks, habits, and deep work.

The Problem: Calendar Tetris

If you're a Business Analyst or PM, your calendar is a warzone. You have 4 hours of meetings, 3 hours of "actual work," and zero contiguous blocks of time to do it. You spend half your day rescheduling things.

Reclaim.ai isn't just a calendar; it's a bouncer for your time. You tell it what you need to do ("Review specs for 2 hours"), and it finds the time. If a meeting gets booked over it, Reclaim automatically moves your task to the next available slot.

Key Features

1. Habits

Set recurring tasks like "Lunch" or "Code Review," and Reclaim ensures they happen, but moves them around conflicts. It's smart enough to know you can't eat lunch during a board meeting.

2. Smart 1:1s

Stop emailing back and forth. Reclaim automatically finds time for recurring 1:1 meetings that works for both people's calendars.

3. Buffer Time

Automatically adds breaks between back-to-back Zoom calls so you can actually breathe or grab water.

Reclaim vs. Motion

We reviewed Motion recently (see our review here). Here's the difference:

  • Motion is a project manager that schedules tasks. It's powerful but complex ($34/mo).
  • Reclaim is a calendar defender. It's simpler, integrates deeper with Google Calendar, and has a generous free tier.

Final Verdict

If you live in Google Calendar, Reclaim is non-negotiable. It saves me about 3-4 hours of scheduling headaches every week. And the free plan is actually usable.
