50 AI Prompts for Business Professionals

Stop staring at a blank page. Here is a curated collection of copy-paste prompts for email, strategy, marketing, and data analysis.

The difference between a generic AI answer and a useful one is the Context. These prompts are designed to give the AI a role, a task, and constraints. Browse by category below.

📧 Email & Communication

Cold Outreach
Act as an expert B2B sales copywriter. Write a 3-email cold outreach sequence to [Target Persona] at [Industry] companies. Goal: Book a 15-minute demo of [Your Product]. Constraint: Keep each email under 100 words. Tone: Friendly professional. Focus on [Specific Pain Point].
Delaying Bad News
Draft an email to a client explaining that the project deadline will be delayed by 3 days due to [Reason]. Tone: Empathetic but firm. Structure: 1. State news directly. 2. Explain reason briefly. 3. Propose new date. 4. Offer small value-add.
Salary Negotiation
I have received a job offer for [Role] at [Company]. The salary offered is [Amount], but my target is [Target Amount]. Write a counter-offer email expressing excitement for the role but referencing my experience in [Key Skill] and market rates to justify the higher request. Tone: Grateful, confident, collaborative.
Meeting Recap
Take the following rough notes from a meeting and turn them into a professional "Meeting Minutes" email. Include: Attendees, Key Decisions, Action Items (with owners), and Next Meeting Date. Notes: [Paste Notes Here]
Polite Refusal (Hiring)
Write a polite rejection email to a job candidate who interviewed for [Role]. They were strong in [Skill A] but we chose someone with more [Skill B] experience. Keep it encouraging and professional.
Introduction Request
Write a short email to [Contact Name], a mutual connection, asking for an introduction to [Target Name] at [Target Company]. Explain that I want to discuss [Topic] and why it would be valuable for them.
Feedback Request
Draft an email to my manager asking for constructive feedback on my recent performance on [Project Name]. Ask specifically about areas for improvement in leadership and technical execution. Tone: Growth-oriented and humble.
Creative OOO
Write a witty and creative Out of Office auto-responder. I am away for [Reason/Holiday] until [Date]. If urgent, contact [Colleague Name]. Include a fun fact or a link to a helpful resource to keep them entertained while I'm gone.
Ghosting Follow-Up
I sent a proposal to a prospect 10 days ago and haven't heard back. Write a gentle "bump" email. Do not sound desperate. Subject line options: "Is this still a priority?" or "Quick check-in". Keep it under 50 words.
Apology for Mistake
I sent the wrong attachment to a client. Write a quick apology email. Format: Acknowledge error, attach correct file immediately, apologize for confusion. Keep it very short.

🧠 Strategy & Planning

Pre-Mortem Analysis
We are launching [Project Name]. Act as a pessimistic risk analyst. Conduct a "Pre-Mortem". Assume the project has failed 6 months from now. List the top 10 reasons why, categorized by (Technical, Market, Operational). For each, suggest one mitigation strategy.
SWOT Analysis
Perform a SWOT analysis for [Company Name/Product], a competitor in the [Industry] space. Based on public knowledge, identify Strengths, Weaknesses, Opportunities, and Threats. Focus specifically on their recent move to [Recent Action/Feature].
Blue Ocean Strategy
I run a [Business Type]. Help me apply Blue Ocean Strategy to find an uncontested market space. 1. What factors does the industry take for granted? 2. What factors should be reduced well below industry standards? 3. What factors should be raised well above industry standards? 4. What factors should be created that the industry has never offered?
First Principles
I am trying to solve [Problem]. Break this problem down using First Principles thinking. Deconstruct it into its fundamental truths or "atomic" elements, disregarding analogies ("how it's usually done"). Then reconstruct a solution from the ground up.
Porter’s 5 Forces
Analyze the [Specific Industry] industry using Porter's Five Forces framework: 1. Threat of new entrants 2. Threat of substitutes 3. Bargaining power of customers 4. Bargaining power of suppliers 5. Competitive rivalry Provide a summary rating (High/Med/Low) for each.
Value Prop Canvas
Create a Value Proposition Canvas for [Product Name] targeting [Customer Segment]. List: - Customer Jobs (what they are trying to do) - Pains (risks, obstacles) - Gains (outcomes they want) Then map our features to these Pains and Gains.
OKR Generator
Help me draft OKRs (Objectives and Key Results) for the [Department Name] team for Q3. The main company goal is [Company Goal]. Suggest 3 Objectives, and for each objective, list 3 measurable Key Results (with numbers/percentages).
Scenario Planning
Imagine 3 future scenarios for the [Industry] market over the next 5 years: 1. Optimistic (Tech boom) 2. Pessimistic (Recession/Regulation) 3. Status Quo For each scenario, describe how our company, which does [Business Activity], should prepare today.
Lean Canvas
Draft a 1-page Lean Canvas for a new startup idea: [Idea Description]. Outline: Problem, Solution, Unique Value Prop, Unfair Advantage, Customer Segments, Key Metrics, Channels, Cost Structure, and Revenue Streams.
Decision Matrix
I need to choose between [Option A] and [Option B]. Create a decision matrix. Suggest 5 criteria to judge them by (e.g., Cost, Speed, Scalability). Assign a weight (1-5) to each criterion. Help me score them hypothetically to see which wins.

📣 Marketing & Content

Blog Post Outline
Create a detailed outline for a 1,500-word blog post titled "[Title]". Target Audience: [Audience]. Include: H2s, H3s, key points for each section, and a suggested "Call to Action" at the end.
Social Media Calendar
Generate a 1-week social media content calendar for LinkedIn for a [Role/Industry]. Topics should rotate between: Industry News, Personal Story, actionable Advice, and Case Study. Format: Day, Topic, Post Hook, and Image Idea.
SEO Meta Data
Write 5 variations of an SEO Title Tag (max 60 chars) and Meta Description (max 160 chars) for a webpage about [Topic]. Keywords to include: [Keyword 1], [Keyword 2]. Make them click-worthy.
YouTube Script Hook
Write 3 distinct intros (hooks) for a YouTube video about [Video Topic]. 1. The "Story" hook. 2. The "Controversial Opinion" hook. 3. The "Direct Promise" hook. Keep each under 30 seconds of speaking time.
Ad Copy Variants
Write 3 Facebook Ad primary text options for [Product]. - Option 1: Focus on FOMO (Fear Of Missing Out). - Option 2: Focus on Social Proof (Testimonials). - Option 3: Focus on Logical Benefit/Savings.
Landing Page H1s
Generate 10 headline (H1) ideas for a landing page selling [Product/Service]. Use different formulas: - The "How to" headline - The "Result in Time" headline - The "Direct Benefit" headline - The "Pain-Agitate-Solve" headline
Customer Personas
Create a detailed customer persona for [Product]. Name: "Manager Mike". Include: Demographics, Job Title, Goals, Frustrations, Brands he loves, and Where he hangs out online.
Newsletter Subject Lines
I am sending a newsletter about [Topic]. Write 10 catchy subject lines. Mix styles: Curiosity gap, Benefit-driven, Urgency, and Personal. Keep them under 50 characters so they don't get cut off on mobile.
Competitor Analysis (Content)
Analyze the writing style of this text: [Paste Text]. Describe the tone, sentence structure, and vocabulary level. Then, rewrite the following paragraph to match that exact style: [Your Paragraph].
Viral Hooks
Turn this boring sentence into 5 viral Twitter/X hooks: Original: "Consistenty is important for success." Make them punchy, use numbers, or challenge conventional wisdom.

⚡ Productivity & Workflow

Pareto 80/20
I want to learn [Skill] fast. Apply the Pareto Principle. What are the 20% of sub-skills that yield 80% of the results? Create a 4-week crash course based on this.
Eisenhower Matrix
Here is my to-do list: [Paste List]. Categorize these items into the Eisenhower Matrix: 1. Urgent & Important (Do now) 2. Not Urgent & Important (Schedule) 3. Urgent & Not Important (Delegate) 4. Not Urgent & Not Important (Delete)
Time Blocking
Create a daily schedule for a [Role] using the Time Blocking method. Work hours: 9 AM to 5 PM. Include blocks for Deep Work, Admin, Meetings, and Breaks. Ensure the most cognitive-heavy tasks are in the morning.
Meeting Agenda
Create a tight agenda for a 30-minute meeting about [Topic]. Allocated time: - 5 min: Context - 15 min: Discussion/Brainstorming - 10 min: Decisions & Next Steps List specific questions we must answer by the end.
Summarizer
Summarize the following text into 3 bullet points. Constraint: Use simple language (Grade 8 reading level). Text: [Paste Text]
ELI5 (Explain Like I’m 5)
Explain the concept of [Complex Topic, e.g., Blockchain] to me like I am a 5-year-old. Use analogies involving toys, food, or playgrounds. Avoid jargon.
SMART Goals
I have a vague goal: "I want to grow my business." Rewrite this into a SMART goal (Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, Time-bound). Give me 3 variations.
Habit Stacking
I want to build a habit of [New Habit]. Suggest 5 "Habit Stacking" recipes using my existing daily routine. Format: "After I [Current Habit], I will [New Habit]." (Current habits: Coffee, brushing teeth, opening laptop, lunch, closing laptop).
Inbox Triage
Act as an executive assistant. I will paste an email. Tell me: 1. Does this require a reply? (Yes/No) 2. Is it urgent? (High/Low) 3. Draft a one-sentence reply if needed. Email: [Paste Email]
SOP Creator
Write a Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) for [Task Name]. Structure: - Objective - Tools Needed - Step-by-Step Instructions (numbered) - Common Pitfalls to Avoid - Definition of Done

📊 Data & Analysis

Excel Formula Gen
I have a spreadsheet with Column A (Date) and Column B (Revenue). Write an Excel formula to sum revenue ONLY if the date is in "January 2024". Explain the logic.
Regex Pattern
Write a Regular Expression (Regex) to extract all email addresses from a text block. Ensure it captures emails with subdomains (e.g., name@mail.server.com). Provide the regex string and a brief explanation.
SQL Query Helper
Write a SQL query to select the top 5 customers by total spend from two tables: 'users' (id, name) and 'orders' (user_id, amount). Use a LEFT JOIN. Order by total spend descending.
Python Pandas Cleaning
I have a pandas DataFrame `df`. Column 'Price' contains strings like "$1,200.50" and "NA". Write a Python snippet to: 1. Remove the '$' and ',' characters. 2. Convert the column to numeric (coercing errors to NaN). 3. Fill NaN values with the median.
Data Viz Ideas
I have a dataset showing [Variables, e.g., Customer Age vs. Spend Amount]. Suggest 3 ways to visualize this data to show the correlation. For each, explain why it is effective and what insight it reveals.
KPI Selection
I manage a [Team Type, e.g., Customer Support] team. Suggest 5 Key Performance Indicators (KPIs) we should track. Avoid vanity metrics. Focus on efficiency and customer satisfaction. For each KPI, define how it is calculated.
Trend Interpretation
I will paste a list of monthly sales figures. Act as a data analyst. 1. Calculate the Month-over-Month (MoM) growth rate. 2. Identify any seasonality or outliers. 3. Summarize the trend in one sentence. Data: [Paste Data]
JSON to CSV
I have a complex nested JSON file. Explain the step-by-step logic (pseudocode) to flatten this into a CSV format where each row is a unique record. Handle arrays within objects.
Sentiment Analysis
Analyze the sentiment of the following customer review. Classify as: Positive, Negative, or Neutral. Extract the main "topic" or feature mentioned. Review: "[Paste Review]"
Explain Metric
Explain the difference between "Mean", "Median", and "Mode" to a non-technical stakeholder. Give a concrete example of when using the Mean would be misleading (e.g., salary data with one billionaire).
