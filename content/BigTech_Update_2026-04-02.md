### OpenAI: ChatGPT Finally Gets Its Hands Dirty
The biggest news out of Sam Altman’s orbit isn't just a smarter brain, but the fact that ChatGPT can finally *do* things outside of its own chat box. 

*   **The Big Deal: Full MCP (Model Context Protocol) Support.** ChatGPT Business now lets your workspace build and publish MCP-powered connectors. This means ChatGPT can now natively read *and write* to your tools. You can tell it to update a CRM record, create a project task in Linear, or pull a report from Notion—and it actually does it. 
*   **Knowledge Cutoff Bump:** GPT-5.2 (across Instant, Thinking, and Pro) just got its knowledge updated to August 2025. 
*   **App Upgrades:** The native integrations for Notion, Box, Dropbox, and Linear just got a refresh with new write capabilities.

**The Founder/BA Takeaway:** If you’ve been duct-taping APIs together with Zapier just to make ChatGPT update a Jira ticket, you can stop now. MCP connectors mean your AI is no longer just an advisor; it’s an operator. BAs should immediately look into building custom MCP connectors for their internal tools.

### Google: Gemini 3.1 Wants to Have a Real Conversation
Not to be outdone, Google rolled out a massive update for the Gemini app centered around Gemini 3 and 3.1. 

*   **Gemini Live on 3.1:** Google claims this is their biggest upgrade to Live yet. It holds context twice as long and flows faster. (Read: you don’t have to repeat your premise three times when brainstorming a pitch deck).
*   **Workspace & Developer Tools:** Deeper hooks into Docs, Sheets, and Drive, plus a handy new "Gemini API Docs MCP" for developers. 
*   **A Fun Trick:** Gemini can now create custom 30-second music tracks from a prompt or a picture. Useful? Maybe for your next TikTok ad. Fun? Absolutely.

**The Founder/BA Takeaway:** Google is doubling down on making Gemini your constant companion. For analysts crunching data in Sheets or founders drafting docs, the tighter Workspace integration is the real money-maker here. And the longer context window in Live makes it an actually viable sounding board for messy, unstructured brainstorming.

We are officially out of the "copy and paste" era of AI. With OpenAI’s MCP rollout and Google’s deep Workspace hooks, the friction between *generating* an idea and *executing* it is disappearing.
