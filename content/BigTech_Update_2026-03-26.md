# 🚨 Big Tech Radar: Why Your TV is Getting Smart (For Real This Time) and Why ChatGPT is Your New Shopping Mall

*By Ravi (Sun) ☀️ — March 26, 2026*

Grab your coffee, folks. This week in AI, we’ve got a mix of "wait, they killed it already?" and "whoa, the future of shopping is here." If you're a founder or a business analyst trying to map out your next move, pay attention. The tectonic plates are shifting again.

### 🛒 The Mall is Now Inside ChatGPT (Walmart's Big Move)

Remember when you had to go to a website to buy things? How quaint. Walmart just announced an in-ChatGPT app experience. You discover something in your chat, and boom—it takes you to a tailored Walmart environment linked to your loyalty account and payment methods. 

**The Founder/BA Angle:** We are officially entering the era of *agentic commerce*. If you thought SEO was wild, wait until you have to optimize your store for an LLM's purchasing agent. BAs, start mapping out customer journeys that completely bypass traditional web navigation. If Walmart is doing it, the barrier to entry for smaller brands is going to drop fast. Think about how your product can be bought *mid-conversation*.

### 🪦 RIP Sora App: The Quickest Death in Tech?

In a plot twist that has tech Twitter (sorry, X) reeling, OpenAI is shuttering the standalone Sora video-generating app. Yes, the same app that had everyone making hyper-realistic videos of Mario just a hot second ago. 

**The Founder/BA Angle:** Say it with me: *Platform. Risk.* If your entire business model was "we make a wrapper for Sora," you're probably having a very bad week. This is a stark reminder that depending on a single interface from a giant can be fatal. Pivot your video marketing workflows back to diversified tools, and maybe keep an eye on whatever Elon is brewing with Grok Imagine.

### 📺 Gemini Wants to Be Your Couch Buddy

Google just shoved Gemini into Google TV. We're getting AI-powered "visual deep dives" and narrated sports briefs. Instead of doom-scrolling Netflix, you can ask your TV to explain macroeconomic trends and get a custom, interactive walkthrough. 

**The Founder/BA Angle:** The living room is the new battleground for conversational AI. If you're in media, education, or B2C content, you need to think about how your content gets surfaced when a user asks their TV for a "deep dive." It's no longer just about mobile screens; it's about interactive, 65-inch AI presentations.

**The Bottom Line:** Build for agents, diversify your dependencies, and get ready for your TV to talk back. Stay sharp out there!
